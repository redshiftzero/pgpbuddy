    What is PGP Buddy?

    PGP Buddy is a tool written by @krasch and @redshiftzero for helping you test and practice using PGP. You can send PGP buddy emails, and she will check that you are doing things correctly. If PGP buddy finds a problem, then she will email you and let you know what the problem was.

    Try PGP buddy: buddy@pgp.today

    Basic Challenges

    Task 1: Getting Started

    Send PGP buddy a regular email at buddy@pgp.today

    Follow the tutorial PGP buddy provides in response to set up PGP on your system.

    Task 2: Signatures

    Send PGP buddy a signed email only

    Signing emails demonstrates that a message was written by the person who is in control of a given keypair. PGP buddy needs your public key in order to verify the signature, so make sure that you attach your public key or put it on a keyserver.

    Check the signature on the signed response from PGP buddy

    Read the email from PGP buddy and verify that the signature checks out.

    Task 3: Encryption

    Send PGP buddy an encrypted email only

    Encrypting emails keeps the content of the emails secret, but does not conceal metadata.

    Decrypt the response from PGP Buddy

    PGP buddy will try to decrypt your email and let you know if there were problems. In addition, PGP buddy will try to encrypt an email back to you. PGP buddy needs your public key in order to encrypt an email to you, so make sure that you attach your public key or put it on a keyserver.

    Task 4a: Putting it together

    Send PGP buddy a signed and encrypted email

    Put everything together by both signing and encrypting an email to PGP buddy.

    Decrypt the email and check the signature on the signed response

    Decrypt and verify the signature on the response from PGP buddy.

    Privacy Policy

    This service is intended as a testing service for setting up PGP only. If messages are successfully handled, we delete that message after we respond to them, and as well as the PGP public key we download after it has been used to generate the response.

    Note on logging: During beta testing, we are logging messages that we fail to respond to appropriately in order to determine what additional situations PGP buddy should be able to handle.
